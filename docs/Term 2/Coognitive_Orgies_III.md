# Coognitive Orgies III
![Image 12](../images/COIII.avif){ align=left }
Instead of treating a diary as a document to write, this project makes it something you inhabit. A dark room that responds to your presence, your voice, your hesitation — projecting back not what you said, but how it moved through you.

## Personal Reflection:
Since the idea behind the project started off as an interactive mindmap - organizing how we would make it and how we would have it react was an interesting experience for sure. How to define a mind - how can we make it unique to the person using it? Those were questions we started off with. And these are the questions that led to it evolving from a mind map to a diary - an archive of sorts of the least tangible forms of us - our thoughts.

## Moral Traces:
I think the largest challenge while designing this was how to allow it to personalize it while also having a common general idea of it working. We explored this through a couple of inputs - audio frequency, audio amplitude, movement of our hands. But the challenge was also to bring an output that matched the same. Here exploring how I change shapes and colors of  our visuals was a lot of trail and error but ultimately so worth it.

## Technical Process Traces:
Here as well as a visual graphic of the words of the user towards the diary we also wanted the words to come on screen. First I started with trying to get a n8n chain that heard- transcribed- interpreted with AI the tone, emotion - gave an output in the form of transcribed words and colors as per the interpreted emotion. This chain in itself took more than a day to perfect, especially when transcribing audio to text. But after finally achieving this - we realized connecting this to touchdesigner where we had our other inputs and graphics was not possible - at least not for free. 

And hence I had to find a way to do the same process in touchdesigner locally. With innumerable tutorials and a lot of help from reddit and Claude when I finally got it- getting the same thing on the other laptop we were working on as a team became a challenge ans the transcribing of touchdesign used local files. So yes a lot of back and forth where in each win somehow became a complication instead of a solution. But using that as a challenge taught me not only so many technical skills across different softwares but also at the variety of ways through which you can explore the same problem.
